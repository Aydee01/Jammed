Lateralus

by Tool

Official Aydee Score

10/10

Lateralus is Tool's third studio album which was released on May 15 2001, it is easily considered by many fans and critics as their best work to date with the usual themes of Tool exploring spirituality and philosophy through their songs and the sheer complexity of their pieces.

Their previous record prior to Lateralus was Ænima and was already considered their magnum opus at the time, with the lengthy period between the release of Lateralus and Ænima many speculated the beginning of the end for the band's stardom. One of the main reasons for the delay was the band's legal dispute with their record label at the time Volcano Entertainment.

One funny tidbit of fact during the album rollout of Lateralus. Tool's usual "Shenanigans" of messing with their fans, though this time there was a more reasonable reason for why they did it, released a totally fake album teaser On January 1st upon the announcement of their upcoming third studio album where they revealed a totally fake album name with 12 fake tracklists to mess with the first wave of piracy and file sharing services which plagued the music industry at the time with .mp3 files.

The whole album overall can almost be described as a deeper understanding of ones self through their own ways in the context of each narrative of the songs, it's an almost "Awakening" from each situations each songs take place in. From the first track for example "The Grudge" is a song about well... Grudges, but specifically maturing and evetually letting it go. The album deals a lot with a Philosophical change whether it's ego death with the grudge, or the celebration from realising the beauty of life and existence with Parabola or Lateralus. It is truly an compact album filled with a bunch ideas that look through life and death, pain and joy, love and hate. It's really a record where you will keep finding something the more you listen to the record.
